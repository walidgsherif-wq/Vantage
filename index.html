Vantage
/ˈvantɪdʒ/  ·  noun  ·  Old French avantage

A position giving a strategic advantage. A place or condition affording superiority, commanding perspective — the elevated point from which everything below becomes clear, readable, and actionable.

In real estate, the broker with the highest vantage point wins. They price correctly. They move first. They close faster. Not because they work harder — because they see further.

From Latin ab ante — "from before." The name was chosen deliberately. Vantage does not put you alongside the market. It puts you ahead of it.
